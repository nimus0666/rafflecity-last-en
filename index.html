The basics — understanding it all
A raffle is a digital lottery. You buy one or more tickets for a chance to win a prize. At the set date, one winner is drawn at random from all participants. That's it. The difference with a classic lottery is that at RaffleCity, the draw is automatic, verifiable by everyone, and the prize goes directly to the winner — no human intermediary.
Imagine a giant ledger shared by thousands of computers around the world. Everything written in it is permanent, visible by everyone, and nobody can erase or modify it. RaffleCity uses Solana, a fast and low-cost blockchain, to record every ticket, every draw and every result. Result: nobody can cheat — not even us.
An NFT (Non-Fungible Token) is a unique digital item recorded on the blockchain. Think of a collectible card, but digital: you truly own it, you can keep it, show it or sell it. At RaffleCity, Founder NFTs are membership cards: they give you permanent benefits on the platform, and you can resell them whenever you want — just like a real collectible card that gains value.
B2C (Business to Consumer): the platform runs raffles directly for regular users. You sign up and try to win.

B2B (Business to Business): companies use RaffleCity for their own needs — launching a product, engaging customers, or clearing stock through draws instead of classic discounts.

P2P (Peer to Peer): individuals organize their own raffles on the platform — like selling something, but as a draw. You set the ticket price and the prize.
A smart contract is an automatic computer program. When the conditions are met, it executes on its own — no human needs to press a button. At RaffleCity, the smart contract manages everything: it counts the tickets, draws the winner, and sends the prize automatically. Nobody can block it or change the result.
RWA stands for Real World Assets — physical items like cars, luxury watches, apartments, artwork that can be put up as raffle prizes. With RaffleCity, you could buy a $10 ticket and win a $50,000 car. The idea: making prizes accessible that you could never normally afford.
